# Positioning — The North Star

Every post — short-form or long-form — must reinforce this identity.

## The Statement

> **AI product guy building a life in Chicago — money, style, dating, ambition.**

It's specific enough to be memorable, broad enough to cover the three short-form buckets and three long-form pillars.

## Why It Works

Most creators in adjacent niches own one slice:
- AI educators (technical, but no lifestyle/personality)
- Lifestyle/dating creators (no professional credibility)
- "Day in life" PMs (no AI edge, generic)

The combination is rare. It also reflects what's actually true — not a persona to maintain.

## Bio (identical across channels)

```
AI product @ [company] · building Cortex · Chicago · life, style, dating, ambition
```

Substitute the company name with whatever's permissible based on day-job confidentiality. If "AI product @ [company]" can't be used, fall back to: `AI PM · building Cortex · Chicago`.

LinkedIn bio extends this with the newsletter link and 1 sentence of value prop.

## Visual Identity

- Same color grade across all videos.
- Same outro card (3 sec, logo + handle).
- Same caption font on reels (sans-serif, white, drop shadow).
- Profile photo: identical across TikTok, IG, LinkedIn, Medium, Beehiiv.

Consistency is what compensates for topic variety across the three buckets.

## Voice Rules

- **Confident, dry, observational.** Not hype. Not preachy.
- **Self-deprecation > grandstanding.** Especially on dating/mindset content.
- **Specific over general.** Numbers, names of bars, real outfits, real prompts.
- **No corporate-speak, no listicle filler, no motivational quotes.**
- **Read every draft aloud.** If it sounds like a bootcamp ad or LinkedIn lunatic, rewrite.

## On-Brand vs. Off-Brand Examples

| On-brand | Off-brand |
|---|---|
| "$120 night at this Chicago bar — worth it?" | "10 ways to spend money smarter" |
| "RAG is dead — what AI PMs should care about in 2026" | "5 trends every PM needs to know" |
| "I don't do coffee dates. If I like you, it's dinner." | "Here are 7 dating tips that changed my life" |
| "We killed a feature this week. Here's why." | "Always ship fast and iterate!" |

## Confidentiality Guardrails

Across both workstreams:
- Never name internal day-job projects, customers, exact comp, or non-public roadmap items.
- Cortex is the safe public artifact — talk about it freely.
- "Patterns and my own decisions" is the maximum specificity for day-job content.
- One trusted reader reviews any borderline piece before posting.

## The AI-Pipeline Angle Is On-Brand

Using AI avatars and AI tooling to build the brand is *itself* on-brand for an AI PM. Lean into it — don't hide it. "I built this entire reel in 4 minutes with AI" becomes a recurring hook, not a defensive disclosure.
