## Problem Statement

Professional operator-creators building across multiple channels lose intent, evidence, approvals, identity, and lessons as work moves between AI chats, research tools, editors, schedulers, and analytics dashboards. Clark Pro is a Mac-first creator operating system that keeps the workflow and the creator model in one inspectable, portable workspace, so automation can add leverage without taking hidden authority or erasing judgment.

## North Star

**North Star:** A creator turns a rough thought into a coordinated body of published work while preserving why it mattered, what evidence supports it, which version was approved, what it cost, what happened afterward, and which lesson deserves to influence future work.

## Scope

### In Scope (Clark Pro 1.0)

- Signed and notarized Mac application with local canonical event and asset ownership
- Focus, Canvas, Timeline, Review, Library, Memory, and Connections over one workspace model
- Durable creation loops from capture through publication, observation, reflection, and improvement
- Governed MCP capabilities, Clark Bridge, Tool Packs, and Agent Skills with explicit permissions and rollback
- Postiz-first distribution with direct, assisted, and deterministic export fallbacks
- Single-user product plus the minimum remote scheduling and workspace boundaries required for safe continuity

### Out of Scope (Clark Pro 1.0)

- Training proprietary foundation models
- Generic workflow automation unrelated to creator operations
- Fully autonomous content farming or silent memory, Skill, strategy, or publication decisions
- Platform-policy circumvention or hidden AI disclosure
- Mandatory cloud ownership of canonical personal state
- Direct Clark-owned integrations for every social platform

## Constraints

- Canonical identity, memory, credentials, project history, and approval state remain local-first and portable.
- Renderer code receives no ambient secrets, shell, raw filesystem, or arbitrary network authority.
- Models and engines remain replaceable behind stable domain and capability contracts.
- Every external mutation is idempotent or enters explicit reconciliation; blind retry is not acceptable.
- Human, commercial, security, migration, rollback, and signing evidence cannot be replaced by passing unit tests.
- Open-source reuse requires immutable source identity, compatible licensing, supply-chain evidence, stable interfaces, permission review, conformance, and rollback.

## Open Questions

- Which supported external engine should replace OpenCut as the first real activated Tool Pack proof if OpenCut does not add a stable integration boundary?
- Which initial model providers, Postiz targets, and direct social connectors satisfy the representative creator's actual channel mix?
- What team, capacity, dates, and binding commercial terms will close the remaining Ground signoff entries?
- Which physical-erasure design satisfies local event history, backup retention, and creator expectations without making unsupported deletion claims?
