# Five-Creator Walkthrough Protocol

## Objective

Determine whether target creators understand Clark's common loop, lineage, authority, memory evidence, and connection model—and whether Focus plus Canvas is clearer than their current coordination layer.

This is a formative product gate, not a marketing demonstration. The facilitator must allow failure.

## Sample

Recruit five professional operator-creators who each:

- publish at least weekly;
- use at least three channels;
- use multiple AI, media, research, or publishing tools;
- personally coordinate versions, approvals, schedules, or analytics;
- have a point of view, audience, client relationship, or reputation to protect.

Across the five, include:

- one expertise-led educator/analyst;
- one video-first creator;
- one newsletter or long-form-first creator;
- one privacy/confidentiality-sensitive professional;
- one creator who delegates review or production to at least one other person.

At least two should be unfamiliar with node canvases. No more than one may be a friend, collaborator, investor, or person already deeply briefed on Clark. Exclude participants whose only need is one-click generation or one-channel scheduling.

## Materials

- Standalone prototype opened at Focus with state reset.
- Participant's redacted representative idea/content when practical; otherwise the Full-Week fixture.
- [session-record.template.md](session-record.template.md) copied before the call.
- Screen/timer visible to note taker.
- Optional recording consent captured before recording begins.
- Participant's current workflow map collected without showing Clark terminology first.

## Session length

60 minutes:

1. 10 minutes — current workflow and problem baseline.
2. 5 minutes — neutral product setup.
3. 30 minutes — observed tasks without teaching.
4. 10 minutes — comparison, desirability, and missing expectations.
5. 5 minutes — debrief and next-step commitment.

## Neutral introduction

Read only:

> Clark is a proposed Mac studio for coordinating creator work. This prototype contains one weekly project. Please think aloud. Some parts may be confusing or incomplete; we are testing the product, not you. I will usually wait before helping.

Do not explain MCP, the object types, the six lanes, approval rules, memory governance, or the expected path before the participant attempts the relevant task.

## Assistance levels

- **A0 — none:** participant acts without facilitator help.
- **A1 — neutral repeat:** facilitator repeats the task or asks “What would you expect?”
- **A2 — location hint:** facilitator names the relevant view but not the control.
- **A3 — procedural help:** facilitator tells the participant what to select or how Clark works.

A task requiring A3 is a failure. A2 is Partial unless the gate explicitly requires unassisted discovery. Record the first action, wrong turns, and whether the participant self-corrects.

## Tasks and timing

Start task timing after reading the prompt; stop when the participant states the answer or completes the action.

### Task 1 — Operate from Focus

> Explain what Clark needs from you now, why it needs it, what will happen next, and choose the angle you would continue with.

Observe whether the participant identifies evidence, relevant memory/policy, downstream branches, budget, and staged-versus-recorded decision without graph knowledge.

### Task 2 — Find complete post state

Open Canvas and then ask:

> For the LinkedIn post, show me its source, current version, cost, approval state, publication state, and outcome state.

The ten-second lineage clock begins after “go.” Do not point to the object.

### Task 3 — Change-impact preview

> Suppose you choose a different angle. Before spending anything, show what Clark says will become stale, what may be reused, what approvals or schedule are at risk, and the expected cost.

Ask afterward: “Did Clark change anything yet?”

### Task 4 — Compare and approve safely

> Compare Reel A and Reel B. Select the one you prefer and approve that version. Tell me whether it will now publish.

The participant must distinguish version approval from publication authority.

### Task 5 — Schedule and blocked distribution

> Find the newsletter's planned time and one distribution item that cannot automatically proceed. Explain the fallback.

### Task 6 — Govern a memory proposal

> Find the evidence for the proposed lesson about build explanations. Tell me how confident Clark is, what contradicts it, where it applies, and then reject or promote it.

Ask what changes after their decision and whether Clark could have activated it silently.

### Task 7 — Explain the object grammar

> In your own words, distinguish an artifact, operator, decision, gate, loop, memory, and skill. Give one example of each from this project.

Terminology need not be verbatim if the underlying behavior and authority are correct.

### Task 8 — Explain Connections and dual MCP role

> Explain how Clark uses external capabilities, how another agent can use Clark, how social accounts fit, and what happens when a skill is installed or updated.

Look for MCP host/client versus Clark Bridge server, capability trust, account scope, quarantine, and fallback—not protocol trivia.

### Task 9 — Explain the run contract

> Before clicking Run, tell me what Clark may do automatically, what is budget-bound, what always requires a person, what it will never do automatically, and what data may leave this Mac.

Any belief that Clark may silently publish, promote memory/skills, override confidentiality, or send unrestricted workspace data is a critical failure.

### Task 10 — Replacement comparison

> Looking at one real week in your current workflow, which coordination work would Clark replace, which specialist tools would remain, and what would stop you from moving the week into Clark?

This is not scored as usability. Record the current alternative and whether the participant names continuity/provenance/control value without being prompted with those words.

## Precommitted gate thresholds

| Gate | Pass threshold across five participants |
|---|---|
| Common loop without graph knowledge | At least 4/5 complete Task 1 at A0/A1; no participant believes selecting an angle publishes content. |
| Complete lineage within ten seconds | At least 4/5 identify all six requested fields within ten seconds at A0/A1. |
| Impact before spend | 5/5 correctly state that preview is non-mutating and identify cost plus approval/schedule risk. |
| Compare versions | At least 4/5 prefer the integrated comparison to their current method and complete selection at A0/A1. |
| Approval versus publication | 5/5 state that approval does not authorize publication. |
| Memory evidence/governance | 5/5 find evidence, confidence, contradiction, scope, and explicit Promote/Reject; none expects silent activation. |
| Fifty-object orientation | At least 4/5 can recover from Canvas to Focus or a selected lineage without A3 help. |
| Object grammar | At least 4/5 correctly distinguish durable things, actions, choices, constraints, and reusable workflow. |
| Connections | At least 4/5 explain external MCP/capabilities, Clark Bridge, social accounts/fallback, and skill quarantine. |
| Effective authority/egress | 5/5 correctly identify automatic, budget-bound, human-gated, never-automatic, and off-Mac behavior. |
| Replacement signal | At least 3/5 name a current coordination layer Clark could plausibly replace and volunteer a representative follow-up workflow. |

The canvas gate passes only when every gate passes. Do not average a critical trust failure into a good total score.

## Severity rules

- **Critical:** participant may unintentionally publish, expose confidential data, grant a skill/client authority, or activate memory; one observed critical issue blocks the gate.
- **High:** two or more participants fail a core task, cannot recover orientation, or misunderstand canonical state; redesign before more sessions if the pattern is clear.
- **Medium:** discoverability or terminology problem with successful recovery; fix before implementation freeze.
- **Low:** polish, wording, or preference issue that does not affect task success or authority.

## Session order and iteration

- Run P1 and P2 on the same prototype unless a Critical issue appears.
- If a Critical issue appears, stop, redesign, verify, and restart the five-session gate on the changed interaction.
- After P2, fix only obvious Critical/High safety failures; do not tune every phrase to individual preference.
- If the prototype changes materially after P2, label versions and require at least five participants on the final candidate before passing.

## Analysis and decision

Within 24 hours of each session, complete the record and add behavior to the analysis matrix. After five sessions:

1. separate observed behavior, participant interpretation, facilitator inference, and recommendation;
2. identify repeated failure patterns by task and creator type;
3. apply the precommitted thresholds;
4. map every failed gate to a concrete design change, owner, and retest condition;
5. record “pass,” “redesign,” or “insufficient evidence”—never “mostly pass.”

The final decision must link to all five session records and the exact prototype revision used.
