# Three-Partner Real-Week Protocol

## Objective

Test whether Clark can become the creator's coordination layer across a representative multi-channel week while specialist tools remain replaceable capabilities. The study must reveal first-week value, recurring value, trust cost, operational failure, and whether governed memory improves the next week without creating review burden.

## Participants

Recruit three design partners from the primary ICP. Each partner must:

- own a real recurring content operation across at least three channels;
- bring representative, publishable work and existing tools;
- have authority to evaluate or buy the product;
- commit to one onboarding/baseline session, four consecutive operating weeks, weekly review, and an end-of-study purchase decision;
- agree on what content may enter providers and what must stay local/redacted.

Avoid three nearly identical creators. Include different dominant formats and at least one privacy-sensitive operation.

## Safety boundary

- Use a separate test workspace and explicit account grants.
- Never request raw credentials through the prototype or ordinary chat.
- Use real publication only when the creator has reviewed the exact version/account/destination and the integration is proven safe; otherwise use validated export/assisted handoff.
- Do not ingest employer/client confidential content without written scope and a tested redaction/local-only policy.
- Memory and skill changes remain proposal-only and visible.
- Record provider spend separately from Clark product value.

## Phase 0 — Baseline

Before Clark, map one comparable week:

- source/idea capture locations;
- tools and handoffs;
- repeated prompts/context reconstruction;
- time spent coordinating rather than creating;
- number of artifacts, versions, channels, and publication attempts;
- approval, scheduling, and publication-state mistakes;
- provider/media spend;
- how outcomes are reviewed and lessons retained;
- creator-rated confidence, effort, and publishability.

Collect artifacts or screenshots only with permission. The baseline can use participant estimates, but mark estimated versus observed.

## Phase 1 — Onboard and import

1. Create the local canonical workspace and backup location.
2. Import or write Brand Constitution, boundaries, audience, channel roles, and current project intent.
3. Connect only capabilities/accounts needed for the first real week.
4. Review egress, autonomy, budget, and publication policy.
5. Import representative sources and existing content history needed for immediate value.
6. Run dry-run; resolve every missing credential, schema, policy, and gate.
7. Confirm what Clark will not replace: editor, design tool, camera, platform backend, or other specialist.

Measure time to trustworthy first plan, not merely time to generate text.

## Phase 2 — Four consecutive weeks

Each week follows the same observed sequence:

1. Capture one or more real intents.
2. Build evidence and retrieve relevant creator context.
3. Choose the content angle/branch.
4. Produce and compare platform artifacts using existing specialist tools through adapters or assisted handoff.
5. Review exact versions, cost, policy, evidence, and disclosure.
6. Schedule/publish or export with explicit final state.
7. Observe platform and creator-judgment outcomes.
8. Inspect reflection output and accept, edit, dispute, defer, or reject each proposal.
9. Begin the next week and identify whether promoted memory/skills reduce reconstruction or improve alignment.

Do not rescue every failure manually outside Clark without logging the workaround; operational gaps are study evidence.

## Weekly measures

### Operation

- elapsed and active time from capture to first reviewable artifact;
- coordination/reconstruction time;
- creator edit time and revisions per approved artifact;
- number of external tools and manual state transfers;
- artifacts/channels completed;
- paid provider cost and cost per approved/published artifact;
- blocked, failed, cancelled, reconciled, and exported publications;
- restart/recovery and connector-fallback events;
- missing information or authority ambiguity.

### Trust

- creator can explain what ran, why, what left the Mac, and what it cost;
- incorrect or noisy policy warnings;
- attempted stale approval or wrong-account action;
- unknown/duplicate publication state;
- credential, scope, memory, or skill concern;
- time spent reviewing automation rather than creating.

### Personalization

- memory items retrieved and whether each was helpful, irrelevant, or harmful;
- proposals accepted, edited, rejected, disputed, or deferred;
- false lesson or overfitting incidents;
- corrections/forget requests and proof of future exclusion;
- skill proposal/test/rollback burden;
- repeated instructions Clark failed to retain or incorrectly retained.

### Replacement value

- prior notes/spreadsheet/chat/scheduler coordination work no longer needed;
- new work Clark created;
- work moved back to the old system;
- unprompted return the following week;
- whether the creator would be materially worse off if Clark disappeared.

## Weekly review questions

Ask after the operation, not before:

1. Where did Clark preserve context you would otherwise reconstruct?
2. Where did it add review or bookkeeping work?
3. Which decision felt more informed because provenance/memory was visible?
4. Which specialist tool or manual handoff was still necessary?
5. Was any state or authority unclear?
6. Which proposal should Clark not have made?
7. What would prevent you from running next week here?
8. If Clark disappeared tomorrow, what would you move back—and where?

## Pass gates

All three partners must complete at least one representative week. Product expansion beyond Ground requires:

- at least 2/3 voluntarily operate all four weeks with real work;
- at least 2/3 reduce recurring coordination/reconstruction work relative to baseline without increasing critical errors;
- no duplicate/unknown publication caused by Clark and no critical confidentiality/authority breach;
- every participant can trace one publication from source through outcome and inspect one memory proposal;
- governed proposal review remains lower effort than the context/rework it prevents;
- at least 2/3 identify Clark as replacing a current coordination layer rather than merely adding another tool;
- purchase evidence meets [purchase-signal.md](purchase-signal.md), or the commercial gate remains open.

Any critical trust incident blocks the study outcome regardless of time savings.

## Weekly record

For each partner/week, create:

```text
partner ID
workspace/fixture revision
intent and selected channels
baseline comparison
run/event/decision/publication receipts
timing/cost/revision measures
failures and workarounds
memory/skill proposal decisions
creator judgment and direct quotes
next-week commitment
```

## Final decision

Choose one:

- **Continue:** first-week value and four-week replacement behavior are strong; remaining issues have bounded fixes.
- **Narrow:** value appears only for one creator format/job; update ICP and product promise before expansion.
- **Redesign:** continuity value is real but Focus/Canvas, memory burden, or connector reliability blocks adoption.
- **Stop:** creators do not change behavior, coordination is not costly enough, or trust/review burden exceeds value.

The decision must reference the baseline, four-week records, actual behavior, critical incidents, and purchase action—not team enthusiasm.
