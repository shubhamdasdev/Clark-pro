# Binding Willingness-to-Pay Protocol

## Objective

Test whether target creators value Clark enough to make a costly, reversible, or contractual decision. Compliments, feature requests, “I would pay,” newsletter signups, and free pilot participation are not binding evidence.

## Prerequisites

Run this only after the participant has:

- completed a representative workflow;
- seen the actual local/remote boundary and provider costs;
- experienced the current limitations and fallback paths;
- identified what Clark replaces and what remains in specialist tools;
- had the opportunity to decline without harming the relationship.

## Package presentation

Present the real intended packaging from `positioning-and-business.md`:

- Clark Studio — complete local Mac product with BYO providers;
- Clark Relay — remote schedules/jobs and managed operational continuity;
- Clark Team — roles, shared review, policies, and encrypted collaboration.

Use product-team-approved candidate prices and billing periods. Do not invent a special low price for each participant after hearing their budget. Provider/model/media spend must be shown separately.

## Acceptable binding signals

In descending strength:

1. paid annual or monthly subscription/order;
2. signed paid design-partner agreement with start date and cancellation terms;
3. refundable deposit credited to first paid term;
4. purchase order or written procurement commitment from the actual budget owner;
5. time-bound card authorization or equivalent checkout commitment that will convert under stated launch conditions.

A calendar booking, verbal yes, product feedback, or letter of intent without economic consequence is supporting context only.

## Test script

After summarizing observed value and limitations, say:

> You have now used the current Clark workflow and seen what it replaces, what remains, provider costs, and the trust model. We are testing an actual paid package rather than asking a survey question. Would you choose one of these packages under these terms today? It is completely fine to decline; the reason is useful.

Then stop talking. Do not immediately discount, add future features, or argue with the objection.

## Record

- participant and budget authority;
- package/price/term shown;
- action offered;
- accepted, declined, or deferred;
- time taken to decide;
- exact objection or condition;
- whether condition concerns price, missing value, trust, timing, procurement, or product scope;
- any discount or custom promise made;
- whether money/contract/authorization actually changed state;
- cancellation/refund later and reason.

## Precommitted commercial gate

Before implementation expands beyond Ground, at least three target creators must take a binding purchase action at a package/price the team could sustainably honor. At least two should do so without a custom feature promise that changes the core product.

If real usage is positive but binding action fails:

- do not reinterpret free engagement as willingness to pay;
- separate price objection from insufficient replacement value;
- test packaging or ICP only when evidence supports the change;
- preserve the product's local ownership and transparent provider-cost principles;
- repeat with a new precommitted offer rather than negotiating until someone says yes.

## Ethical boundary

- Deposits must have clear refund conditions.
- Participants must understand what exists versus what is planned.
- Do not pressure creators using relationship, scarcity, or access to their own exported data.
- Never bundle consent to use private content with the purchase decision.
