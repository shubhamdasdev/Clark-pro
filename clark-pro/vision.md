# Clark Pro — Product Vision

## Product Definition

Clark Pro is a **Mac-first, memory-native creator operating system**. It turns intent into published work through durable, visible loops and builds an inspectable model of the creator from their decisions and results.

The Mac app is the first and primary product because a creator's real working context already lives there: notes, screenshots, recordings, drafts, media, browser sessions, credentials, calendars, and editing tools. Clark should feel like a studio that belongs on the creator's computer, not another website asking them to upload their life.

## The Problem Has Three Layers

### 1. Workflow fragmentation

Ideas, research, scripts, media, drafts, approvals, publishing, and analytics live in separate tools. Context is lost at every boundary, and changing an upstream decision produces manual rework.

### 2. Identity fragmentation

Each AI session receives a partial prompt pretending to be a brand. The creator's voice, boundaries, taste, recurring beliefs, audience, strategic goals, and prior corrections are not maintained as a coherent, inspectable model.

### 3. Learning fragmentation

Performance dashboards show outcomes but do not connect them to the exact source, angle, hook, prompt, model, edit, approval, and platform adaptation that produced the outcome. Lessons remain anecdotal or disappear.

Clark solves all three as one system. Workflow without memory becomes automation. Memory without workflow becomes chat history. Analytics without provenance becomes weak correlation. The value is in connecting them.

## Target User

### Primary: the professional operator-creator

A solo creator or founder who:

- publishes across three or more channels;
- has a real point of view and reputation to protect;
- uses multiple AI and media tools;
- wants leverage without surrendering judgment;
- needs repeatable production, not one-off generations;
- values ownership, privacy, provenance, and portability.

This is a narrower and better initial customer than “all creators.” It includes build-in-public founders, expert creators, professional educators, and high-output personal brands.

### Later expansion

- Creator teams and agencies that need shared approval and account boundaries.
- Brands that need policy, evidence, and auditability.
- Builders who want a programmable creator harness through MCP and Clark Kit.

## The Product Promise

A creator can begin with a rough thought and finish with a coordinated body of published work without losing:

- why the idea mattered;
- which claims are supported;
- which version was approved;
- which tool produced each asset;
- how much it cost;
- where it was published;
- what happened afterward;
- what lesson, if any, deserves to change future behavior.

## The Three Core Feelings

1. **“It knows my operation.”** Clark understands identity, audience, active projects, tools, constraints, and working preferences without forcing the creator to repeat them.
2. **“I can see and control the work.”** Every meaningful decision, artifact, agent action, approval, failure, and cost is visible and interruptible.
3. **“It improves without drifting.”** Clark proposes memories and skills from evidence; the creator can approve, edit, reject, expire, or forget them.

## The Differentiation

Clark does not claim to be the only visual AI workspace or the only social MCP integration. Those categories already exist. Clark wins by combining four properties deeply:

| Property | Clark's position |
|---|---|
| **Personal continuity** | A structured, inspectable creator model—not a prompt suffix or opaque vector store. |
| **Operational continuity** | Durable loops survive restarts, reconnect to async jobs, and preserve approvals, budgets, and lineage. |
| **Creative provenance** | Every artifact traces back through sources, choices, skill versions, tools, models, edits, and outcomes. |
| **Protocol openness** | Clark is an MCP client, an MCP server, an Agent Skills host, and a capability SDK without making any protocol its internal domain model. |

The durable moat is not model access, a list of integrations, or a blank node editor. It is the creator's accumulated **decision graph**: the connected history of intent, evidence, artifacts, judgment, distribution, outcomes, and promoted lessons.

## The Clark System

### Clark Studio

The Mac application with six coordinated views over one underlying project graph:

- **Focus** — the next decision or task, without canvas complexity.
- **Canvas** — lineage, branches, dependencies, loops, and live execution.
- **Timeline** — production and publishing across time.
- **Review** — approvals, comparisons, policy warnings, and cost.
- **Library** — projects, artifacts, templates, sources, and past outputs.
- **Memory** — what Clark believes, why it believes it, and how to change it.

### Clark Harness

The durable local runtime. It compiles a creative graph into run plans, executes tools and agents, schedules work, enforces policy, records events, recovers after interruption, and evaluates results.

### Clark Memory

The creator model with separate identity, semantic, episodic, procedural, and performance layers. Retrieval is context-specific; mutation is evidence-bound and reviewable.

### Clark Connect and Clark Bridge

Clark Connect consumes MCP servers, direct APIs, local tools, and skills. Clark Bridge exposes Clark's projects, memory, workflows, review queue, and publishing capabilities as a permissioned MCP server to other agents.

## The Core Loops

Clark is organized around durable loops rather than isolated features.

1. **Capture loop:** collect an idea, source, note, recording, link, screenshot, or observation and connect it to the right project.
2. **Sensemaking loop:** research, verify, retrieve relevant memory, identify gaps, and develop angles.
3. **Production loop:** create, compare, edit, render, validate, and assemble artifacts.
4. **Distribution loop:** adapt, approve, schedule, publish, verify, and recover through connector fallbacks.
5. **Observation loop:** collect platform outcomes, comments, qualitative signals, and creator judgment.
6. **Reflection loop:** separate signal from noise, connect outcomes to lineage, and propose memories or strategy changes.
7. **Skill loop:** turn successful or corrected procedures into versioned, testable Agent Skills with an approval gate.

Every loop has an entry contract, success contract, budget, permission policy, checkpoints, evidence requirements, recovery behavior, and observable state.

## Product Principles

### Artifacts over conversations

Chat can guide work, but the durable units are ideas, sources, decisions, scripts, media, posts, runs, outcomes, memories, and skills.

### Evidence over confidence

Clark may recommend an angle or lesson, but it must show the evidence and uncertainty. It must never present small-sample social performance as causal truth.

### Guided first, programmable always

The common path begins with templates and Focus mode. Advanced users can open the full canvas, edit loops, install capabilities, and call Clark through MCP.

### Human judgment is training data only with consent

Edits, approvals, rejections, and corrections are valuable signals. Clark records them as evidence but does not silently turn them into permanent beliefs.

### Local ownership with optional execution elasticity

Canonical identity, memory, credentials, and project history stay on the Mac. Cloud workers may render or run scheduled jobs using scoped, revocable leases.

### Replaceable intelligence

Models are selected per task through a provider-neutral gateway. No core product behavior depends on one vendor's agent SDK.

## Boundaries

Clark is not:

- a generic automation builder;
- a model-training company;
- a social network;
- a platform-policy bypass tool;
- a fully autonomous content farm;
- a blank infinite canvas that requires creators to become workflow engineers;
- an opaque “growth score” system pretending correlation is causation.

## North-Star Outcome

After six months, Clark should hold a portable, inspectable operating model of the creator: what they are building, who they serve, how they sound, how they work, what they refuse to do, which procedures are reliable, which claims are supported, which content decisions have evidence, and where uncertainty remains.

The ultimate product is not a machine that posts more. It is a studio that preserves and compounds creator judgment.
