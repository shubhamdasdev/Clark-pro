# Clark Studio — Five-Workflow Cognitive Walkthrough

**Date:** July 12, 2026  
**Method:** expert, task-based rehearsal against the clickable prototype. This is not participant research and does not count as the five required human walkthroughs.

## Why this pass exists

Before asking creators for time, the prototype should survive obvious workflow differences and expose its own authority model. Five representative creator operations were walked through using the same canonical Full-Week project rather than inventing five unrelated product modes.

## Workflow set

| Workflow | Primary concern | Representative task | Pre-change result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Expertise-led solo creator | Preserve judgment while producing across four channels | Choose one evidence-backed angle, approve a reel, and schedule adaptations | Partial — Focus was clear, but the selected post did not expose the whole provenance contract in one place. |

| Small creator team | Separate content approval from account authority | Hand off review without granting publication or personal memory access | Partial — approval separation was explicit; roles, assignments, and team memory remain a later synchronization stratum. |
| Privacy-sensitive professional | Know what leaves the Mac and what may act automatically | Run research/generation while protecting confidential inputs and social accounts | Partial — policies and connection scopes were visible, but the effective run/autonomy contract was scattered. |

## Cross-workflow findings

### 1. Provenance must be a readable contract, not just graph edges

The canvas gate requires an expert to identify source, current version, cost, approval state, publication state, and outcome lineage for any post within ten seconds. The original inspector showed only kind, state, and a short detail. Graph relationships alone did not satisfy the gate.

**Resolution:** every selected object now opens a consistent inspector contract. Representative publication artifacts include concrete upstream source, canonical version, cumulative cost, approval, planned/live state, outcome state, and effective permission. A `Trace source to outcome` action keeps the nearby lineage visible.

### 2. Memory evidence needs contradiction, scope, and confidence

A one-line evidence count makes promotion look safer than it is. Creators need to distinguish an explicit identity rule from a weak outcome correlation and see contradicting evidence before promotion.

**Resolution:** every memory item now has `Inspect evidence`. The inspector separates evidence, confidence, contradiction, scope, and retrieval state. Promotion and rejection remain independent human actions.

### 3. “Autonomy” should be expressed per action class

A global autonomy slider would hide the product's most important trust boundary. The creator needs to know what can run, what is budget-bound, what always asks, and what Clark will never do automatically.

**Resolution:** Focus now includes a run contract and remote-data summary. Connections includes the effective autonomy matrix and the permission intersection used by the harness.

### 4. One canonical project is stronger than persona-specific modes

Adding an “educator mode,” “video mode,” or “agency mode” would fragment the model. The same artifact, decision, gate, loop, memory, and capability grammar covers the workflows. Templates and policies should vary; the domain model should not.

### 5. Team collaboration should not distort the Ground canvas

Roles, assignments, encrypted synchronization, and shared approval queues are real whole-product requirements. They are deliberately built as a higher cumulative stratum because they change topology and authority. The Ground prototype must prove individual creator value and canonical state first; it should not fake multi-user collaboration.

## Decision

The prototype is ready for five real creator walkthroughs. The expert pass removed three avoidable failures, but it does not prove comprehension, speed, desirability, or replacement value. The canvas gate remains open until observed creator behavior passes the evaluation rubric.
